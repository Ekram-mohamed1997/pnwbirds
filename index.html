Black-headed Grosbeak.

The Black-headed Grosbeak is known for caroling like a tipsy sounding robin. It has a black head, butterscotch feathers along the body, with black and white wings. It is a part of the Cardinalidae bird family tree. They are known for singing and being domestic parents. Both males and females sit on eggs, feed their chicks and defend their nesting territory. When breeding the Black-headed Grosbeak can be found in both Central and Western regions of the United States. Throughout the whole year they can be found in all areas of Mexico. You can often find them eating off of feeders because they love seeds, especially Sun Flower seeds. They love nests in cooler breezy areas, hence a tree that is leafy is preferred for their nests. Because of their massive bills, they are great at cracking seeds. These beaks are also utilized in catching insects and hard bodied bugs such as beetles. When migrating, they mainly feed off of berries, poison oak and juneberries. When the summer comes around, they can be found feeding off of sunflowers or feeders in backyards and parks.

Chestnut-Backed Chickadee.

Apart of the Paridae Family, a Chestnut-Backed Chickadee is a species to look for across the west coast, mainly seen in the Pacific Northwest. They are known to be hyper, sociable, active and loud. They prefer woody areas but will adapt well in suburban neighborhoods as well. They have husky short bodies with rounded heads and a dark caramel coat. Chickadees will be found mainly in the Pacific Northwest in high branches of coast lines or even lower park areas. Their calls make a chickadee sound. So, if you're trying to find one, especially in the winter, listen for that sound. They will come to homes with bird feeders and even inhabit an area where nests are created to their liking. This species of birds relies heavily on protein in their diet. They enjoy spiders, caterpillars and leafhoppers. Every so often they will feed off of seeds berries and fruit too. They'll respond well to feeders that provide mixed seeds.

White Crowned Sparrow.

A White Crowned Sparrow is an intelligent black and white headed, pale beak and gray bodied bird. They love flocked through bushy borders and high grown fields. They are known for their thin sweet whistling. This bird comes from the Passerelidge family of birds, known for eating seeds. Alongside with seeds that they mainly feed off of, they too enjoy caterpillars, wasps, beetles and many more insects, especially during the summer. They're quite the health nut because they enjoy oats, barley, corn and fruit as well. If you would like to find this bird, their nests are usually found on the ground hidden amount mats of moss. They are found across the United States especially during Autumn in fields, roadsides and trail edges. If you hope to see some in your backyard, be sure to look during the months of March and April. Having feeders will be helpful too.

Golden Crown Sparrow

A common bird in weedy areas or shrubby lowlands, even found in city edges along the Pacific Coast. They tend to leave their homes during the summer for chilly places like Alaska or British Columbia. With a husky built, this bird is also gray, with a half gray and black head topped with yellow feathers down the middle. These birds haven't been tested on much in labs, so what we known of them are from general observations. They can be spotted anywhere from Baja California to Alaska. They spend Winters in Baja California or Nevada. They then migrate to shrubby Tundras near Alaska or Canada. They nest on the ground with ferns, grasses and fobs. Occasionally, you can spot their nests in small trees. The Tundra is known for not having trees, that's why their nests are usually grounded. The Golden Crown Sparrow will eat seeds, fruits, buds and flowers. Sometimes they feed off of vegetables such as cabbages, beets and peas. For protein they'll include ants, moths, beetles and termites in their diet. They'll forage with a mate or alone.

Hairy Woodpecker

The Hairy Woodpecker may appear in many regions but it is an uncommon bird. They are apart of the woodpecker family ranging in the larger scale. They usually appear as black and white birds with the occasional red feather on top of its flat shaped head. It is recognized for being well adapted to pecking and climbing. They're easy to research and observe and they easily come to feeders. If there aren't feeders, they tend to be shy. The male Hairy Woodpecker are usually visible in fields. The Hairy Woodpecker is a North American bird. There for it can be spotted in all three regions of North America. They can appear anywhere from Mexico, The United States and Canada. They prefer to nest in woody and leafy areas on top of tree trunks. The Hairy Woodpecker is a smart bird. They feed off of where they live. Before they decide where they nest, they test tress by pecking on them and see which tree offered the most insects. They love beetles, worms, ants and many more. On rare occasions they feast on caterpillars, wasps and spiders! The Hairy Woodpecker prefers participating in family dinners, they eat with a huge number of other Hairy Woodpeckers at a time. From all of that, they have helped with pest control in the forests of their region. For healthy carbs and sugars, they eat fruits and seeds. Fun fact, babies are known as being loud eaters.
